Adelin of Séez (also, Hadelin, Adelheim, Adelhelm, or Adalhelmus) (? - c. 910) was a Benedictine monk and abbot at the abbey of Anisole.[1] He was the Bishop of Séez for twenty-six years starting in c. 884.[2] He is noted for authoring a work on the life and miracles of Saint Opportuna (Vita et miracula Sanctae Opportunae).[3]
