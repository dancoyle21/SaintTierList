Enodoch

Jump to navigation Jump to search

The church in the golf course

Saint Enodoch, also known as Wenedoc, is a 6th-century Pre-congregational saint of South Wales.

Enodoch is believed by some historians to have been a woman, called Qendydd.[1] She died about 520AD.[2] and her feast day is 7 March.[3]

St Enodoc's Church, the parish church of Trebetherick in Cornwall is dedicated to this saint, and its churchyard is home to the grave of John Betjeman.
