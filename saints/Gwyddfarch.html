Gwyddfarch

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to navigation Jump to search

Gwyddfarch was a hermit and founder of a Celtic abbey at Meifod in Wales.[1] He was a son of Amalarius and disciple of St. Llywelyn at Welshpool. About 550AD he founded a monastery [2] at Meifod. This establishment became the mother church of several other monasteries and was a centre of the order for over a 1000 years, and within a generation the monastery had become a centre of pilgrimage.

Gwyddfarch taught Tysilio,[3] who replaced him as abbot.[4][5]

Legend holds that near the end of his life Tysilio talked the ageing abbot out of a pilgrimage to Rome.[6] He is commemorated on 3 November.[7]
