Gwenafwy

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to navigation Jump to search

Saint Gwenafwy (fl. 6th c.) was a pre-congregational saint of medieval South Wales.[1] She was a daughter of Caw of Strathclyde,[2] and sister of Peillan, Eigron and Peithein among others.[3] She went to Cornwall with her brother Saint Eigron where she is remembered in churches at Gwennap.[citation needed]

Her feast day is 1 July.[4]
