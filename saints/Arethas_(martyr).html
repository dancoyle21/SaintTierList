Arethas or Aretas (Arabic: الحارث‎ "al-Ḥārith") was the leader of the Christian community of Najran in the early 6th century, was executed during the persecution of Christians by the Jewish king Dhu Nuwas in 523.[1][2] He is known from the Acta S. Arethae (also called Martyrium sancti Arethae or Martyrium Arethae)[3] which exists in two recensions: the earlier and more authentic, which was found by Michel Le Quien (Oriens Christianus, ii. 428) and was subsequently dated as no later than the 7th century; the later, revised by Simeon Metaphrastes, dates from the 10th century. The Ge'ez and Arabic versions of the text were published in 2006[4] and the Greek version in 2007.[5]

Feast day: 27 July (Roman Catholic Church) [6] In Eastern Orthodox Church his feast day is 24 October (6 November O.C.).[7]
